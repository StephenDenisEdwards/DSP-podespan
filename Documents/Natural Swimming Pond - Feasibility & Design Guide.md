## 3. Feasibility Assessment

### Factors in Favour

1. **Plenty of space** — 740 m2 of green area; a natural swimming pond typically needs 50-150 m2 (swimming zone + regeneration zone)
2. **Deep water table (7 m)** — no risk of groundwater contamination or buoyancy/flotation of an empty pond
3. **Stiff clay soil (F5 ML)** — naturally low permeability; clay soils are the best natural base for ponds, potentially reducing or eliminating the need for an artificial liner
4. **No public stormwater sewer** — rainwater is already managed on-site; a pond could integrate with or supplement the existing retention/infiltration strategy
5. **Sloping terrain** — allows gravity-fed circulation between regeneration and swimming zones; enables naturalistic cascading design
6. **Granite-derived soil** — chemically neutral to slightly acidic; suitable for aquatic planting

### Factors to Address

1. **Soil permeability (kv = 5 x 10-6 m/s)** — while the clay is relatively tight, this permeability is not zero. For a permanent pond, you likely need:
   - A **compacted clay liner** (min. 300 mm, ideally from the on-site clay) to achieve kv < 10-8 m/s, OR
   - A **bentonite mat** or **EPDM/butyl rubber liner** as a waterproof barrier
   - Testing recommendation: commission a Proctor compaction test on the site clay to confirm it can achieve adequate seal when compacted

2. **Terrain slope** — excavation on a slope means unequal cut depths; the downhill side will have a significant exposed wall. Retaining solutions or naturalistic grading (shelving, planted slopes) needed.

3. **Fill material to 1.80 m** — the upper soil is anthropogenic fill (navazka), not undisturbed ground. Pond excavation should ideally reach into the natural deluvial clay (Layer III, below 1.80 m) for a stable, lower-permeability base.

4. **Existing utility routes** — the site has long utility runs (electricity 82 m, water 81 m, sewer 40 m, rainwater 57 m). The pond must clear these with adequate setbacks. Check C.3 for exact routes.

5. **Radon** — the site has a **HIGH radon index** (72.3 kBq/m3 at 75th percentile). This is relevant to the house foundations but not directly to an open-air pond (radon dissipates freely in open water/air). However, if you build any enclosed plant rooms or pump chambers underground, they would need radon protection.

6. **Setbacks from boundaries** — Czech building regulations typically require setbacks for water features. Check the local uzemni plan (zoning plan) for Jevany and consult the stavebni urad (building authority) about whether a natural swimming pond requires ohlaseni (notification) or stavebni povoleni (building permit).

7. **Winter conditions** — Jevany is at ~400 m ASL in central Bohemia; expect hard frosts. The pond needs sufficient depth (min. 1.8-2.0 m in the swimming zone) to avoid freezing solid, and the circulation/filtration system must be winterizable.

## 4. Design Considerations for a Natural Swimming Pond

### Sizing

A natural swimming pond (prirodni koupaci jezirko) typically has two zones:

| Zone | Purpose | Typical ratio |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Swimming zone (plavecka zona) | Deep, clear water for bathing | 50-70% of total area |
| Regeneration zone (regeneracni zona) | Shallow, planted area for biological filtration | 30-50% of total area |

**Minimum recommended total area:** 50 m2 (very small); 80-120 m2 is more practical for good water quality.

Given 740 m2 of available green space, a **100-150 m2 pond** is entirely feasible while preserving ample garden.

### Depth

| Zone | Recommended depth |
|------|-------------------|
| Swimming zone | 1.80-2.50 m (2.0 m minimum for diving prevention + frost depth) |
| Regeneration/planting shelves | 0.10-0.60 m |
| Transition slopes | 1:3 gradient maximum for safety |

### Excavation Volume Estimate

For a 100 m2 pond with average depth 1.5 m:
- **Excavation volume:** ~150 m3
- **Spoil:** F5 ML clay — potentially reusable for compacted clay liner, landscaping berms, or grading the slope
- Note: excavation will pass through the fill layer (0-1.80 m) and into natural clay below

### Water Supply

- **Initial fill:** ~150 m3 for a 100 m2 pond = 150,000 litres
- The site has a water supply connection (PE-MD 40x5.5 mm) — filling from mains is possible but slow at 0.01 l/s peak demand; consider using a temporary larger-diameter connection or filling over several days
- **Top-up:** Natural ponds lose water to evaporation (~5 mm/day in summer = 500 l/day for 100 m2). Rainwater harvesting from the roof (currently going to soakaway) could offset this
- **Rainwater integration opportunity:** The existing system collects from 250 m2 of roof. Instead of sending all rainwater to the soakaway, some could be diverted to the pond for top-up, reducing mains water dependency

### Circulation & Filtration

Natural swimming ponds rely on:
1. **Biological filtration** — aquatic plants in the regeneration zone (reed beds, water lilies, submerged oxygenators)
2. **Mechanical circulation** — low-energy pump(s) moving water between swimming and regeneration zones
3. **Optional:** gravel biofilter, skimmer, UV clarifier for additional water quality assurance

The site's slope is an advantage here — water can flow by gravity from a higher regeneration zone down to the swimming zone, with a single pump returning it uphill.

**Electrical supply:** The site has an electricity connection. A small circulation pump (50-150W) can be fed from the house supply or a dedicated outdoor circuit. The existing elektroinstalace drawings (D.1.4.3) show the electrical layout.

### Waterproofing Options

Given the site soil (F5 ML, kv = 5 x 10-6 m/s):

| Option | Suitability | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Compacted on-site clay** | Good if testing confirms kv < 10-8 m/s after compaction | Cheapest; most natural; uses excavated material |
| **Bentonite mat (GCL)** | Excellent | Self-healing; ~5-10 mm thick; reliable waterproofing |
| **EPDM rubber liner** | Excellent | 1.0-1.5 mm; standard for garden ponds; 20+ year life |
| **Concrete with waterproof coating** | Overkill | Expensive; not typical for natural ponds |

**Recommendation:** Test the on-site clay first (Proctor compaction + permeability test on remoulded samples). If it seals adequately, use compacted clay as the primary barrier, potentially with a bentonite mat as backup in critical areas.

## 5. Regulatory & Practical Checklist

### Before Design

- [ ] Check **uzemni plan obce Jevany** (municipal zoning plan) for restrictions on water features, maximum zastavena plocha (built-up area), minimum zelena plocha (green area)
- [ ] Confirm whether a natural swimming pond requires **ohlaseni stavby** (building notification) or **stavebni povoleni** (building permit) with the local stavebni urad
- [ ] Check **minimum setback distances** from parcel boundaries for a water feature (typically 2-3 m)
- [ ] Confirm the existing soakaway (vsakovaci objekt) location — the pond should not be placed above or adjacent to it
- [ ] Verify utility routes on C.3 — mark exclusion zones for all underground pipes

### Soil & Water Testing

- [ ] Commission a **geotechnical investigation** specifically for the pond location (borehole to 3+ m, with permeability testing)
- [ ] **Proctor compaction test** on site clay to confirm liner viability
- [ ] Consider a **trial pit** at the proposed pond location to verify the soil profile matches borehole S1

### Design Phase

- [ ] Survey the proposed pond area in detail using the **10225.dxf** survey data as the base
- [ ] Design pond geometry on the DXF — swimming zone, regeneration zone, shelves, slopes
- [ ] Calculate **cut/fill volumes** from the terrain model
- [ ] Design waterproofing (clay liner, GCL, or EPDM)
- [ ] Design circulation system (pump, pipework, regeneration zone planting plan)
- [ ] Plan **overflow drainage** — where excess water goes during heavy rain (connect to existing soakaway?)
- [ ] Consider **rainwater diversion** from existing roof drainage to pond for top-up
- [ ] Plan electrical supply for circulation pump
- [ ] Design safety measures (fencing if required, depth markers, edge treatment)

### Integration with Existing Project

- [ ] Coordinate with **SO-03** (destove vody / stormwater) — potential to redirect some roof runoff to pond
- [ ] Ensure pond does not conflict with **SO-05** (zpevnene plochy / paved areas, terrace)
- [ ] Check **fire hazard zone (PNP)** from C.3 does not conflict with pond placement
- [ ] Verify adequate distance from **soakaway** (vsakovaci objekt, min. 5 m from buildings per geological report)
